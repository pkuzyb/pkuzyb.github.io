Overview
My research interests lie in sound aspects of language, i.e., phonetics and phonology. I primarily investigate phonological patterns and the articulatory, acoustic, and perceptual aspects of speech sounds in the world's languages. Especially, I am interested in tones and laryngeal registers (voice quality). Phonological phenomena that intrigue include harmony systems, like "tense/lax" laryngeal register and harmony in Nuosu Yi and vowel height harmony in Tibetan, and various linguistic tones, such as consonant tones, syllabe-level tones in some sinitic languages and word-level tones in some Tibetic languages.

Another main focus of my research is to explore the nature of phonological representations and processes invovled in speech production and perception from the perspective of cognitive neuroscience. I view phonological representations as being shaped by various cognitive units such as articulatory gestures (atomic action units), acoustic-auditory features, and visual orthographic symbols, etc.

Furthermore, I believe that speech and language processing builds heavily upon other cognitive abilities, like motor control, perception, attention and memory.

My approaches to these questions include linguistic fieldwork, behavioral paradigms, instrumental phonetic techniques, neuroimaging techniques (mainly electroencephalography) and computational modelling.
