My research interests lie in sound aspects of language, i.e., phonetics and phonology. I primarily investigate phonological patterns and the articulatory, acoustic, and perceptual aspects of speech sounds in the world's languages. Especially, I work on tones and voice quality. Phenomena that have been intriguing me include the so-called 'pitch-accent' in Japanese and some Tibetic languages, lexical tone and tone sandhi in some Sinitic languages, and 'tense/lax' laryngeal register and register harmony in some Loloish (Yi) languages.

Another focus of my research is to explore the nature of phonological representations and mental processes invovled in speech production and perception. I view phonological representations as being shaped by various cognitive units such as articulatory gestures, auditory percept of the acoustic signal, and visual symbols employed in a writing system, etc. Furthermore, I believe that language processing builds heavily upon other cognitive abilities, like motor control, perception, attention and memory.

My approaches to these questions include linguistic fieldwork, behavioral paradigms, instrumental phonetic techqniues, and neuroimaging techniques (mainly electroencephalography).
